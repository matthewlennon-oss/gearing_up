# Bookwork Notes: Wheels and Axles

Copy the notes below into your science book.

## Title: Wheels and Axles

Learning intention: To explain how a wheel and axle can change force or speed.

## Key Terms

Wheel: the larger circular part of the simple machine.

Axle: the rod or shaft attached to the centre of the wheel.

Effort: the force applied by a person or motor.

Load/resistance: the force the machine must overcome.

## Important Idea

A wheel and axle are attached, so they turn together. They complete the same number of rotations.

## Force Multiplier

When effort is applied to the larger wheel, a wheel and axle can multiply force at the axle. This can make a task feel easier, such as turning a doorknob, screwdriver handle or steering wheel.

## The Trade-Off

A force multiplier does not make work disappear. A smaller effort force is applied over a greater distance.

## Speed Multiplier

When effort is applied to the axle, the outside edge of the wheel travels farther in the same time. This can increase rim speed, such as in a motor-driven ceiling fan.

## Diagram

Draw and label a wheel-and-axle system. Include:

- wheel
- axle
- effort force
- load/resistance force

## Summary Sentence

A wheel and axle can make a task easier by trading force, distance or speed, but it does not create extra energy.
